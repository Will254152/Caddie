How to use Pocket Caddie

Your personal golf GPS, scorecard, and shot tracker. All data stays on your phone.

🚀 Quick start

1. Allow location when asked — your blue dot appears on the satellite map.
2. Tap the map anywhere (the green, a bunker) to drop a flag. The big number is live yards from you to it.
3. Tap Mark shot right before you swing to log where you hit from. Tag the club if you want (skippable).
4. Count strokes and putts with +/−, swipe holes with ‹ ›.
5. After the round: Scorecard tab → Save round.

📏 GPS & yardages

The yardage plate shows the distance from your position to your tapped flag, updating as you walk. Tap 📍 to re-center on yourself. Phone GPS is accurate to roughly 3–5 yards — great for club selection, too coarse for short putts. The flag is just a measuring target; move it as often as you like.

⛳ Marking shots

Mark shot records your GPS position instantly, then opens a sheet to tag the club, miss type (chunked, skulled…), and a note — all optional; Skip keeps the shot untagged. The first mark on each hole is labeled Tee. Lines between dots show the distance of each shot. Tap any dot to edit, re-tag, or delete it. ↺ undoes the last mark (with a confirmation — GPS points can't be recreated). ⋯ → Clear hole wipes a hole's marks.

🟥 Drops & free relief

Ball moved? Use Ball moved. For a penalty drop, pick the type — Point of entry (drop near where the ball crossed into trouble or lies unplayable), Drop area / other side (a designated drop zone, or dropping across the obstacle), or Redo (stroke & distance: replay from the previous spot, e.g. re-teeing after OB) — then the penalty strokes (0 / 1 / 2, default 1; 0 is for casual play) and an optional reason. The strokes are added to your score automatically and the shot that caused the drop is flagged in your data. Free relief (GUR/maintenance, cart path, casual water, embedded ball) marks the spot with no stroke.

🧮 Scoring

Strokes is your total for the hole. The putts counter adds to the stroke total automatically, so score = swings + putts. The Tee / FW / GIR chips cycle ✓ → ✗ → blank (FW hides on par 3s). Pars are editable on the Scorecard tab.

🥅 Putting notes

Tap the putts number (dotted underline) to open putting notes: first-putt length, a row per putt for miss direction and comments, plus read and green conditions. You can also GPS-mark putts like normal shots (tag club = Putter) — useful for long lag putts, less so inside 10 feet where GPS error is bigger than the putt.

🤝 Scramble

Scorecard tab → New round → Scramble, enter player names. Marking a shot then also asks whose ball it was (skippable). One team score per hole.

🗂 Saving, history & maps

Save round stores the round on this phone. The Rounds tab lists them; Map reloads a past round's shot trail onto the satellite view; ⬇ downloads a formatted scorecard. Your in-progress round auto-saves constantly — if the browser reloads mid-round you'll be offered a resume.

💾 Your data & backups

Everything lives in this browser's storage on this phone — nothing is uploaded anywhere. Export all data (JSON) on the Rounds tab downloads every round with full detail (GPS points, clubs, tags, notes); it's your backup and your analysis file. Downloaded scorecards also contain the raw data embedded inside. ⚠️ Clearing Safari website data deletes saved rounds — export first.

🔧 Tips

⋯ menu: Sun mode (bright-light display), Edit my bag (customize club list), Clear target pin. Add the site to your Home Screen (Share → Add to Home Screen) to use it like an app. GPS drains battery — start rounds fully charged. If an update doesn't appear, the version number in the header tells you what you're running.
